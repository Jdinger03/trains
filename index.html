Trains

The Sydney Trains network is a suburban passenger rail network serving Sydney, Australia. The network is operated by Transport for NSW, a statutory authority of the New South Wales Government. The network consists of nine lines and over 170 stations.

The train lines are named after their destination: T1 North Shore & Northern, T2 Inner West & Leppington, T3 Bankstown, T4 Eastern Suburbs & Illawarra, T5 Cumberland, T6 Carlingford, T7 Olympic Park, T8 Airport & South, and T9 Northern Line.

The train network also includes the Sydney Metro, a fully automated rapid transit system that currently operates one line, the Sydney Metro Northwest. Two more lines, the Sydney Metro City & Southwest and the Sydney Metro West, are currently under construction.

Buses

The bus network in Sydney is operated by Transport for NSW and includes over 300 routes, with more than 12,000 stops. The network is divided into regions: Eastern Suburbs, Inner West, Northern Beaches, North Shore, Southern Sydney, Western Sydney, and South West.

The buses in Sydney are a mix of double-decker and single-decker buses, and many buses are equipped with free Wi-Fi, USB charging ports, and air conditioning. The network also includes several dedicated bus rapid transit corridors, such as the Metrobus system.

Ferries

The Sydney Ferries network is a public transport network consisting of nine commuter ferry routes and two tourist ferry routes. The ferries are operated by the government-owned corporation, Sydney Ferries. The network serves the Sydney Harbour, Parramatta River, and Georges River.

The ferry network operates a mix of modern and historic vessels, including the iconic green and yellow ferries. Many of the vessels are equipped with amenities such as onboard cafes, bike racks, and free Wi-Fi.

Light Rail

The Sydney Light Rail network is a light rail system that serves the inner western suburbs of Sydney. The network consists of two lines: the L1 Dulwich Hill Line and the L2 Randwick Line.

The network uses a fleet of Alstom Citadis X05 light rail vehicles, which are low-floor and air-conditioned, and feature free Wi-Fi, USB charging ports, and real-time passenger information displays. The network also features modern stations with level boarding, platform screens, and ticket vending machines.

The L1 Dulwich Hill Line runs from Dulwich Hill to Central, and has stops in suburbs such as Lewisham, Petersham, and Glebe. The L2 Randwick Line runs from Circular Quay to Randwick and Kingsford, and has stops in suburbs such as Surry Hills, Moore Park, and Kensington.

The light rail network connects with other public transport modes, including trains and buses, at several interchange stations such as Central, Dulwich Hill, and Circular Quay.
